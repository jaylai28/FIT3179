Unemployment Rates in the United States

A visualisation by Wei Jian Lai

The unemployment rate doesn't just impact those individuals who are jobless, it adversely affects the disposable income of the families as well as affecting those who are still employed. This will create a cascading effect that ripples through the economy. Here's more information on the unemployment rates in the united states, and what factors have affected the country's economy. The visualisation below shows the unemployment rate in the united states in the past 20 years, from 2000 to 2020. Three major economic downturns have occurred during these years.

  

The Dot-Com Bubble Burst

The Dot-Com Bubble occurred between 1995 to the early 2000s where the investors pumped money into internet-based startups in the hopes that these fledgling companies would soon turn a profit. The bubble started to collapse in 1999 causing the unemployment rate to go up on the following few years.

The Global Financial Crisis

The Global Financial Crisis occurred between mid 2007 to the early 2009. It was the bursting of the United States housing bubble and the subsequent subprime mortgage crisis. The downturn in economic activity took effect earlier in the United States causes many people to lose their jobs.

The COVID-19 Recession

The COVID-19 Recession occurred in early 2020. It is currently the worst global economic crisis since the Great Depression. This had caused the unemployment rate to increase drastically across the country, especially from March to April 2020.
